About the First Transcontinental Railroad (FCRR)

On May 10, 1869, a telegram message was sent out accross the nation stating "Done". And at that moment, The First Transcontinental Railroad united the Pacific and Central Railroads. For 152 years, the railroad has been open to the public, transported them from Iowa to Californa. When the First Transcontinental Railroad was built, it was one of the greatest inventions of all time, and was commererated with 4 13 karet golden spikes being planted in the railroad. Currently, 3 of the spikes are in musuems and are mantained by the US goverment
